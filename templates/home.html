What does Your Result Mean?

If you have a footprint value (pounds per year) less than 6000, this is excellent! If you footprint value is between 6000 and 15999, this is a good value. If you have a footprint value between 16000 and 22000, this is average. Anything over 22000 is a poor footprint value. Higher values imply that your are polluting more.

How is this result calculated?



The book The Environment Equation Alexandra Shimo-Barry explains how to quickly calculate your own carbon footprint, which is the amount of greenhouse gases in units of carbon dioxide.
Shimo-Barry uses an equation which takes into account several inputs that require energy, multiplies it by a specific constant, and adds up all the inputs which makes up your carbon footprint value. This calculation does not take the food you eat (which requires a significant amount of energy to grow, process, and deliver) into account. Read more about the impact of different types of foods in the foods section.
